Our Mission

Reading Time: 2 minutes
April 30th, 2019

The Indian Zeal is an explanatory and opinion magazine that aims to foster an informed and responsible populace, and pushes for the much needed progressiveness in the Indian society.

In a nation where primitiveness, prejudice, stigma, and irrational conformity thrive without being challenged, The Zeal aims to fight this paradigm by upholding the very antidotes to social regression: logic, rationality, and the truth.

What We Focus On

Our weekly column will broadly focus on two key areas: socio-cultural issues and the environment.

The pervasive adherence to prejudiced conventions, norms and traditions has restricted free thought and hindered societal progression. The Zeal's content on social and cultural issues challenges such detrimental conformity and also fosters discussions that enlighten the Indian populace.

As climate change and problems concerning sustainability threaten our future now more than ever before, we believe that it is of paramount importance that we ignite discourses on the health of our environment. The Zeal advocates for politicians and policy makers to heed nature's cries and weave environmental solutions into the political and legislative fabric.

Why We Have a Seperate Student Column

It is during the university and college phase when India's youth must be empowered with fundamental liberties in order to become a responsible citizen. Sadly, this is almost never the case. It comes as no surprise that most Indian colleges have an autocratic and patriarchal conduct when dealing with student issues. Students all over the country are often subjected to undemocratic administrations who often tend to use their authority to shun students' of their free speech and other fundamental rights.

The Zeal aims to change this—not by whining and complaining about how unjust the current scenario is—but by holding institutions and authorities accountable for their bigotry. The "Uncovered" series will focus on institutions and those who are responsible for sustaining draconian policies and fostering oppressive environments.

The Student column will also focus on putting forth ideas and issues that deeply affect the student community in the country.

The Zeal Code

All of our opinions, explanations, and other articles are within the realm of veracity. Opinions are backed with firm research and observed statistics, while explanations are underpinned with an unyielding goal of objectivity. The Zeal has a firm policy of not heeding speculation and unreliable sources.

How We're Going to Balance the Scale

Although based on facts, our opinions may be—at their core—biased. Instead of looking at this as a problem, The Zeal perceives this as an opportunity to ignite more discussion and debate. For each and every one of the pieces published on The Indian Zeal, we invite you, the readers, to send us your thoughts and opinions so that we can throw the spotlight on the other side of the coin. Such submissions will be published in the 'Dissent' series, and will be tagged along with the targeted article. We encourage submissions to have a minimum word limit of 400 words.
